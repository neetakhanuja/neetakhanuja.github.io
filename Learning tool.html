Tool for Proactive Learning

An academic project at National Institute Of Design, India.
Intention of this project was to explore solutions that interface theories taught to children in textbooks as the part of curriculum and the environment around them. Attempt was to make learning interesting for them and to give a new perspective to their thinking process.
Just educating children with the contents of their textbooks make learning a tiring job for them but knowledge from textbooks is necessary and needs consideration. So an intermediate solution solving both the purposes that is knowledge and fun learning becomes important.
Reactive and proactive thinking:
In schools it is very easy to put worksheets, blackboard, tests and textbooks in front of students. Students are then asked to react to what is before them. For this practical reason almost all thinking taught in school is reactive. Real life involves great deal of proactive thinking. That means going out and doing things. All information is not given you have to find things.
In this project, a linking between Fibonacci series and nature was explored.
Following are the snapshots of the concept movie.
Final Concept: A handheld device with RFID reader and camera. Interaction starts with storytelling using a medium of 2d animation and a simple game. To continue the story forward and to go to the next level of game, child is supposed to proactively look for the tokens with an embedded RFID tags.
Discovering tokens from surrounding will make children explore whats around them. A story and a game will be completed only when tokens of all elements involved in the story are found. Badges with each level will be given to encorage their interaction.
